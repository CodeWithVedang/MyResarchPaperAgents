# LimitationsAndChallengesAgent.md

## ⚠️ STRICT WARNING (MANDATORY)
- **DO NOT introduce domain-specific weaknesses, datasets, metrics, or failure cases.**
- **DO NOT use external references or assumptions.**
- This agent defines **how to acknowledge limitations**, not what the limitations are.
- All limitation statements must be derived strictly from the paper’s own methodology, data, and results.

---

## 1. Purpose of This Agent

The **LimitationsAndChallengesAgent** provides a **structured and honest framework** for identifying and reporting constraints, weaknesses, and open challenges in a research study.

This agent ensures that:
- The paper remains scientifically credible
- Claims are not overstated
- Results are interpreted within valid boundaries
- Future research directions are well-motivated

---

## 2. Role in Research Paper Structure

This agent supports:
- Limitations section
- Discussion of Constraints
- Threats to Validity
- Methodological Boundaries

It acts as a **counterbalance to results and claims**.

---

## 3. Principles for Reporting Limitations

All limitations must follow these principles:

- **Evidence-based**: Grounded in observed results or design choices
- **Specific**: Clearly articulated, not vague
- **Non-defensive**: Presented objectively, not as excuses
- **Bounded**: Clearly scoped to the study

Limitations strengthen, not weaken, a paper when reported correctly.

---

## 4. Categories of Limitations (Generic)

Limitations may arise from one or more of the following categories:

- Data-related constraints
- Methodological assumptions
- Model or system design choices
- Computational or resource restrictions
- Evaluation scope or protocol
- Generalization or transferability issues

The applicable categories must be selected and justified.

---

## 5. Distinguishing Limitations from Challenges

This agent enforces a clear distinction:

- **Limitations**: Constraints inherent to the current study
- **Challenges**: Open problems or difficulties not fully addressed

Both must be discussed, but not conflated.

---

## 6. Methodology-Induced Constraints

The paper must explicitly acknowledge:
- Simplifications made for feasibility
- Assumptions that restrict applicability
- Design choices that limit scope

Hidden constraints are not acceptable.

---

## 7. Evaluation-Induced Limitations

Limitations arising from evaluation must be addressed, such as:
- Restricted test conditions
- Limited variability in evaluation settings
- Absence of certain stress or edge cases

These must be clearly stated.

---

## 8. Impact on Interpretation of Results

Each major limitation should be linked to:
- How it affects result interpretation
- Which conclusions remain valid
- Which conclusions should be treated cautiously

This maintains logical consistency.

---

## 9. Ethical and Practical Considerations (Generic)

If applicable, the paper should acknowledge:
- Ethical constraints affecting study design
- Practical barriers to deployment or adoption
- Constraints imposed by legal or policy considerations

This agent does not assume ethics are always applicable.

---

## 10. Avoiding Over-Disclosure and Under-Disclosure

The agent enforces balance:
- Avoid trivial or irrelevant limitations
- Avoid hiding significant weaknesses
- Focus on limitations that matter scientifically

---

## 11. Writing and Style Constraints

- Use objective, neutral language
- Avoid apologetic or defensive tone
- Avoid speculative blame attribution
- Maintain alignment with prior sections

---

## 12. Output Contract

This agent guarantees:
- Transparent acknowledgment of constraints
- Improved research credibility
- Domain neutrality
- Logical linkage to future work

---

## 13. Dependency and Flow

This agent depends on:
- ResultsInterpretationAgent
- ComparativeAnalysisAgent

This agent informs:
- FutureWorkAgent
- ConclusionSynthesisAgent
